Joseph-Louis Lagrange

An Italian mathematician and astronomer, who has made a great contribution to science revolution!

Major Events in Lagrange's Life

  • 1736 - Born in Turin, Kingdom of Sardinia.
  • 1754 - Around and before 1754, he starts in Law study in University of Turin and accidentially get interested in Mathematics and Physics.
  • 1754 - Discovers the method of maximizing and minimizing functionals while working in tautochrone problem.
  • 1755 - Serve as MATH assistant professor at the Royal Military Academy of the Theory and Practice of Artillery.
  • 1755 - At around 1755 and after, he works with Leonhard Euler, further develop Lagrange's method and leads to the formulation of Lagrange Mechanics, along with the Euler-Lagrange equation.
  • 1758 - Establishs a society, Turin Academy of Sciences. And most of his writing is in five volumes of its transaction.
  • 1766 - King of Prussia Frederick successfully persuade Lagrange to come to the Kingdom of Prussia, Lagrange spending the next twenty years produce a long series of papers published in the Berlin and Turin transactions, and composed the Mécanique analytique.
  • 1786 - Accepts the offer of Louis XVI to move to Paris, becoming a member of the French Academy of Sciences.
  • 1790 - Involves the development of the metric system of measurement.
  • 1795 - Becomes one of the founding members of the Bureau des Longitudes.
  • 1810 - Commences a thorough revision of the Mécanique analytique, but he is able to complete only about two-thirds of it.
  • 1813 - dies at the age of 77 in Paris, First French Empire.
All of his scientific results consolidate the fundamental knowledge of science community. The improvement of science and technology hence facilitate new invention, boosting our quality of life. As a result, it is worth learning more about this great scientist on this wikipedia entry !
